The Complete Journey - 4000KM for Solar Energy

In the scorching summer of 2024, I, Pankaj Kumar, embarked on a 4000+ km solo journey from Kashmir to Kanyakumari to raise awareness about pollution, global warming, and the power of sustainable energy. Spanning 60 days, the expedition covered 33 cities where I engaged with students, professors, army personnel, and officials—sharing the message of clean energy.

Traveling 100–250 km daily, I battled heatwaves, thunderstorms, and the challenges of solo travel to ignite conversations on solar power as a viable and essential solution for a greener India.

The Challenges

Facing extreme weather conditions like 50°C heat and thunderstorms, the physical strain was immense. But the toughest challenge was traveling alone—managing logistics, safety, and planning every step without support.

I began each day at dawn to minimize heat exposure, learning resilience through every obstacle India’s summer threw at me.

The Milestones

33 cities, 60 days, and thousands of conversations later—every stop mattered.

Over 60 days, I visited 33 cities and conducted sessions in schools, universities, and army camps. Every interaction—from professors in classrooms to soldiers at army bases—was a milestone. These dialogues helped build awareness about solar energy’s potential, turning my personal mission into a national movement of environmental consciousness.

The Impact

My journey touched thousands of lives, sparking discussions about climate change and the role of sustainable energy. People realized that solar energy isn’t just environmentally responsible but also a smart financial decision. This momentum led to the launch of Solaride, where we now help homes and industries switch to solar.

Most residential and commercial solar systems pay for themselves within 4-7 years, depending on your electricity consumption, local sunshine hours, and applicable subsidies. After the payback period, you'll enjoy free electricity for the remaining 20+ years of the system's life. Our systems come with performance warranties of 30 years, ensuring long-term benefits.

We use a calculation where 1kW of solar capacity offsets approximately 0.7 tonnes of CO2 annually. Each mature tree absorbs about 25kg of CO2 per year. Therefore, each 1kW of solar power equates to roughly 28 trees in terms of carbon sequestration. For every system we install, we contribute to tree planting initiatives based on this ratio, enhancing the overall environmental benefit of going solar.

Solar systems require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning of panels (2-4 times per year) is recommended to maintain optimal efficiency. We offer annual maintenance contracts that include thorough inspections, cleaning, and performance checks. Most components have no moving parts, making the systems highly reliable. Inverters typically need replacement after 10-12 years, while panels last 30+ years with proper care.

The Indian government offers several incentives including subsidies of 20-40% on residential installations through the MNRE schemes, accelerated depreciation benefits of 40% for commercial installations, and net metering policies in most states that allow you to sell excess power back to the grid. We help our customers navigate these incentives to maximize their benefits and reduce the initial investment cost.

We use Bifacial N-type Photovoltaic panels with efficiency ratings between 20-24%. These premium panels offer better performance in varied light conditions and higher energy output per square meter. We partner with reputable manufacturers like Adani, Vikram Solar, and Waaree to ensure quality and reliability. Each installation is customized based on your specific requirements and location parameters.

Typically, a 1kW solar system requires about 6-8 square meters of roof space. For an average 5kW residential system, you'll need approximately 30-40 square meters of unshaded area. The exact requirement depends on the panel efficiency and your energy needs. Our technical team conducts a detailed site assessment to determine the optimal system size and layout for your property.

Batteries are optional and depend on your specific needs. Grid-tied systems without batteries are more affordable and work well if your area has reliable grid power and net metering. If you experience frequent power outages or want to maximize self-consumption, a battery system is recommended. We offer various storage solutions like Lithium Ion or Lead Acid Batteries to suit different budgets and requirements.

We account for seasonal variations when designing your system. Our calculations factor in reduced output during monsoon months to ensure year-round reliability. We use advanced mounting systems with proper waterproofing and implement additional ground fault protection. Our panels are tested for high humidity conditions.

We offer multiple financing options including outright purchase, low-interest solar loans through our banking partners, etc.

We handle the entire subsidy application process for you. Our team completes all paperwork, submits applications to the relevant government agencies, and follows up until the subsidy is approved. We ensure all installations meet government specifications to qualify for maximum benefits.

Net metering allows you to send excess solar electricity back to the grid and receive credits on your utility bill. During periods when your system produces more than you consume, the extra electricity is exported. When you need more power than your system produces (at night or on cloudy days), you draw from the grid using your accumulated credits. We handle the net metering application process and coordinate with your local electricity provider for meter installation.

Solar panels do have a carbon footprint during manufacturing, but this "carbon debt" is typically repaid within 1-2 years of operation. After this period, the system generates truly clean energy for its remaining 28+ year lifespan.
